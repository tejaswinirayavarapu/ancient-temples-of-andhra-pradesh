Ancient Temples of Andhra Pradesh

Andhra Pradesh has many wonderful temples that are filled with history, beauty, and tradition. These temples are peaceful places where people come to pray and celebrate festivals. They also show the art and culture of the region.

Exploring the Temples of Andhra Pradesh

Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple Tirumala

The Sri Venkateswara Swami Temple, also known as Tirumala Temple or Tirupati Balaji Temple, is a revered Hindu shrine located in the Tirumala Hills, part of the Seshachalam range in the Tirupati district of Andhra Pradesh, India. Perched at an elevation of 853 meters, the temple sits atop the seventh hill, Venkatadri, and is often referred to as the Temple of Seven Hills

Kanaka Durga Temple Vijayawada

Kanaka Durga Temple, officially known as Sri Durga Malleswara Swamyvarla Devasthanam, is a Hindu temple dedicated to Kanaka Durga. The deity in this temple is also popularly referred as Kanaka Durga. The temple is located in Vijayawada, Andhra Pradesh, India

Mallikarjuna Temple Srisailam

The Mallikarjuna Swamy Temple, also called the Srisailam Temple, is a Hindu temple where people worship the gods Shiva and Parvati. It is in the town of Srisailam, in the state of Andhra Pradesh, India.

Sri Varasidhi Vinayaka Swamy Temple Kanipakam

Sri Varasidhi Vinayaka Swamy Temple is a Hindu temple of Ganesha. It is located at Kanipakam in Chittoor district of Andhra Pradesh, India. The temple is about 11 km from Chittoor and 68 km from Tirupati.

Sri Varaha Lakshmi Narasimha temple Simhachalam

Sri Varaha Lakshmi Narasimha temple, Simhachalam, is a Hindu temple situated on the Simhachalam Hill Range, which is 300 metres above the sea level in the city of Visakhapatnam, Andhra Pradesh, India.

Sri Venkateswara Swamy Temple Tirumala

The temple is dedicated to Venkateswara, a form of Vishnu, who is believed to have appeared on earth to save mankind from trials and troubles of Kali Yuga. Hence the place is also known by the name Kaliyuga Vaikuntha and the deity here is referred to as Kaliyuga Prathyaksha Daivam. The temple is also known by other names like Tirumala Temple, Tirupati Temple and Tirupati Balaji Temple. Venkateswara is also known by other names including Balaji, Govinda, and Srinivasa. The temple is run by Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams (TTD), which is under control of Andhra Pradesh Government. The head of TTD is appointed by Andhra Pradesh Government. Tirumala hills are part of Seshachalam Hills range. The hills are 853 metres (2,799 ft) above sea level and comprise seven peaks, representing the seven heads of Adisesha. Kanaka Durga Temple Vijayawada

Sri Kanaka Durgamma Temple is believed to be a Swayambhu (self-manifested deity). During the month of Sraavanam, Varalakshmi Vratam is performed on all Fridays with devotion, drawing over 20,000 visitors. Dasara (Navaratri) is celebrated grandly every year with special pujas. Thousands of devotees gather, take a holy dip in the Krishna River, and worship the ten forms of Kanaka Durga over the ten-day festival. The ten forms are:

  • Swarna Kavachalankrita Durga Devi
  • Bala Tripurasundari Devi
  • Annapurna Devi
  • Gayatri Devi
  • Lalita Tripura Sundari Devi
  • Saraswati Devi
  • Mahalakshmi Devi
  • Durga Devi
  • Mahishasura Mardini Devi
  • Raja Rajeswari Devi

Mallikarjuna Temple Srisailam

Srisailam holds great religious importance for followers of both Shaivism and Shaktism, two major sects within Hinduism. This sacred site is home to a revered temple that is recognized as one of the twelve Jyotirlingasâ€”holy shrines where Lord Shiva is worshipped in the form of a radiant lingam. At the same time, it is also considered one of the eighteen Shakti Peethas, which are powerful centers dedicated to the worship of the Hindu goddess. In this temple, Lord Shiva is worshipped as Mallikarjuna, while his consort, Goddess Parvati, is honored as Bhramaramba. According to legend, Shiva and Parvati once set out to find suitable brides for their sons, Ganesha and Kartikeya. Ganesha was married to Riddhi (symbolizing intellect) and Siddhi (symbolizing spiritual power). When Kartikeya returned and learned of this, he became upset and chose to live alone on Mount Krauncha in Palani, adopting the name Kumara Brahmachari. When Shiva tried to console him, Kartikeya attempted to leave again, but the gods (Devas) requested him to stay nearby. As a result, Shiva and Parvati remained in the area, and the place where they stayed came to be known as Srisailam. This story adds a deep mythological and emotional layer to the spiritual significance of the temple.

Sri Varasidhi Vinayaka Swamy Temple Kanipakam

Sri Varasidhi Vinayaka Swamy Temple is a Hindu temple of Ganesha According to legend, there were three brothers who were mute, deaf and blind. They were digging a well to fetch water to their field. The device they were using fell into the well hitting hard object. When they dug further, blood started to gush out of the well and the three got rid of their disabilities. The villagers rushed to the spot and found deity of Ganesha. Villagers dug further, but they were not able to find the base of the deity. The deity sits in the well which is always full of water.The idol has apparently gotten larger over time. This is evidenced by the fact that a silver armour of the deity from 50 year ago, doesn't fit today. Currently only the knee and abdomen of the deity is visible.

Sri Varaha Lakshmi Narasimha Temple Simhachalam

Sri Varaha Lakshmi Narasimha temple is dedicated to Lord Vishnu, who is worshipped there as Varaha Narasimha. As per the temple's legend, Vishnu manifested in this form (lion's head and human body) after saving his devotee Prahlada from a murder attempt by the latter's father Hiranyakashipu. Except on Akshaya Tritiya, the idol of Varaha Narasimha is covered with sandalwood paste throughout the year, which makes it resemble a linga. The temple was built in Kalingan architecture styles and stands unique in the historical region of Kalinga. The present temple was built by Eastern Ganga dynasty king Narasingha Deva I in the 13th century and consecrated by his son Bhanudeva I in 1268 CE. The temple was built by Akthayi Senapati, on the command of Narasingha Deva I.
